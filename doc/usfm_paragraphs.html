p

\p(_Sample text...)

* Normal paragraph.

* Followed immediately by a space and paragraph text, or by a new line and a verse marker.

Example of use (TEV Mrk 1):

\c 1
\s The Preaching of John the Baptist
\r (Matthew 3.1-12; Luke 3.1-18; John 1.19-28)
\p
\v 1 This is the Good News about Jesus Christ, the Son of God.
\v 2 It began as the prophet Isaiah had written:
\q1 ?God said, ?I will send my messenger ahead of you
\q2 to open the way for you.?
\q1
\v 3 Someone is shouting in the desert,
\q2 ?Get the road ready for the Lord;
\q2 make a straight path for him to travel!??
\p
\v 4 So John appeared in the desert, baptizing and preaching.
?Turn away from your sins and be baptized,?

m

\m(_Sample text...)

* Flush left (margin) paragraph.

* No first line indent.

* Followed immediately by a space and paragraph text, or by a new line and a verse marker.

* Usually used to resume prose at the margin (without indent) after poetry or OT quotation etc. (continuation of previous paragraph).

Example of use (TEV 1Co 1.20):

\v 18 For the message about Christ's death on the cross is
nonsense to those who are being lost; but for us who are being
saved it is God's power.
\v 19 \x - \xo 1.19: \xt Isa 29.14 (LXX).\x* The scripture says,
\q1 ?I will destroy the wisdom of the wise
\q2 and set aside the understanding of the scholars.?
\b
\m
\v 20 So then, where does that leave the wise? or the scholars?
or the skillful debaters of this world? God has shown that this
world's wisdom is foolishness!
\p
\v 21 \x - \xo 1.21: \xdc Ws 13.1-9.\x* For God in his wisdom made
it impossible for people to know him by means of their own wisdom.

pm

\pm_

* Embedded text paragraph.

Example of use (NIV Act 15.24-29):

\pmo Greetings.
\b
\pm

authorization and disturbed you, troubling your minds by what
they said.

pmc

\pmc_

* Embedded text closing.

Example of use (NIV Act 15.29):

\v 29 You are to abstain from food sacrificed to idols, from
blood, from the meat of strangled animals and from sexual
immorality. You will do well to avoid these things.
\pmc Farewell.
\p
\v 30 The men were sent off and went down to Antioch, where
they gathered the church together and delivered the letter.

pi#

\pi#(_Sample text...)

* Numbers should only be included when more than one level this marker is used within the project text.

mi

\mi_

* An indented flush left paragraph.

* No first line indent

* See also \pmo and \pmc.

cls

\cls_text

* Closure of an epistle.

* Similar to "With love," or "Sincerely yours,".

li

\li(_Sample text...)

* List tem.

* An outdented paragraph meant to highlight the enumerated items of a list.

* Lists may also be used to markup the elements of a recurrent structure, such as the days within the creation account, or the Decalogue (10 commandments).

* \li = \li1 - This version may be used when only one level of \li is in use within the project. See \li# below

pc

\pc(_Sample text...)

* Centered paragraph.

* Recommended use: For (scriptural) inscriptions.

Example of use (CEV Rev 7.5 & NRSV Zec 12.1):

\v 4 The woman was dressed in purple and scarlet robes, and she
wore jewelry made of gold, precious stones, and pearls. In her
hand she held a gold cup filled with the filthy and nasty things
she had done.
\v 5 On her forehead a mysterious name was written:
\pc I AM THE GREAT CITY OF BABYLON, THE MOTHER OF EVERY IMMORAL
AND FILTHY THING ON EARTH.
\m
\v 6 I could tell that the woman was drunk on the blood of God's
people who had given their lives for Jesus. This surprising sight
amazed me,

pr

\pr(_Sample text...)

* Right-aligned paragraph.

* DEPRECATED: Use is strongly discouraged.

* Recommended alternative: See \pmr - Embedded text refrain.

ph

\ph(_Sample text...)

* Indented paragraph with hanging indent.

* DEPRECATED: Use is strongly discouraged.

* Recommended alternative: See \li - List item.

* \ph = \ph1 - This version may be used when only one level of \ph is in use within the project. See \ph# below

ph#

\ph#(_Sample text...)

* See \ph above.

* The variable # represents the level of overall paragraph indent.

* Numbers should only be included when more than one level this marker is used within the project text.

b

\b

* Blank line

* May be used to explicitely indicate additional white space between paragraphs

* See also Poetry Elements \b (used for stanza breaks in poetry, or between poetry and prose).

* No text should follow this marker, and it should not be used before or after titles.
